99.97% uptime in 2025.

Not an aspiration. A number we measure every 60 seconds, published every month, and tied to our team's bonus.

Here's what that actually means for our customers:

- Downtime budget: 2h 37min across the year.
- Real downtime: 2h 12min, across 4 incidents.
- Mean time to recovery: 33 minutes.

"But is uptime a competitive advantage?" No. Uptime is the floor. Below 99.9%, you lose deals. Above 99.9%, nobody notices — unless you're below 99.9%.

What we actually compete on:

1. Time to first ship for a new customer (we measure this in days, not months).
2. Time to resolution on support tickets (24 min median, P95 at 3h).
3. Predictability of our roadmap (every quarterly release lands within 48h of the announced date — 11 quarters in a row).

Boring numbers. No growth hacks. No AI-powered-blockchain-quantum. Just reliability, measured, reported, and paid for.

We think boring is the new exciting. Because if your CI platform surprises you, something has already gone wrong.
