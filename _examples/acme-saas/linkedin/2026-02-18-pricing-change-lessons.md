We raised prices 22% in January. Lost 4 customers. Gained net €340k ARR.

Three things we did that I'd do again:

**1. We announced 60 days in advance.**
Not 30. Sixty. Customers had time to budget, push back, and renegotiate. Half of them renewed early to lock in old pricing. That was fine — it signaled trust in the product.

**2. We grandfathered every annual customer until their renewal date.**
No exceptions, not even for contracts signed 3 weeks before the announcement. Zero surprise. Zero "but we just signed."

**3. We published the rationale.**
Not "market conditions." The real rationale: our infrastructure costs grew 40% in 2 years, and we'd been absorbing it by squeezing margin. At some point that stops being sustainable. Saying that out loud was uncomfortable but fair.

One thing I'd change: we didn't pre-brief our top 5 customers. Two of them heard about the raise from their own finance teams before hearing from us. That's on me. Should have been a personal call a week before the public announcement.

Net result: 96% retention, higher ARR, clearer financial story. The 4 we lost were mostly customers already looking for reasons to leave. The price raise was the trigger, not the cause.
